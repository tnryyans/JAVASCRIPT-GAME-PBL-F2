Our Development Journey

Concept Phase March 13

Brainstorming the initial idea for Shark Feast during school breaks. Sketched first prototypes on paper.

First Prototype Mar 15

Created a basic HTML version with simple mechanics. Tested with classmates for feedback.

UI Overhaul Mar 25

Redesigned the interface with professional graphics and animations. Added sound effects.

Beta Testing April 19

Released to 50 testers. Collected feedback and fixed critical bugs before launch.

Official Launch April 24

Public release of Shark Feast with all planned features implemented.

Behind the Scenes

Developing Shark Feast wasn't just about coding—it was a journey of learning, creativity, and teamwork. We spent countless hours after school and on weekends refining every aspect of the game. From heated debates about game mechanics to celebrating small victories when a particularly tricky bug was fixed, every moment contributed to making Shark Feast what it is today.

One memorable moment was when we stayed up until 3 AM trying to implement the shark's special attack animation, only to realize the next morning that we had accidentally made the shark do backflips instead of biting. It became an inside joke
